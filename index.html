Academic Background

Xinghui Zhong received her B.S. degree in Mathematics from the University of Science and Technology of China in 2007. In 2012 she received her Ph.D degree from the Division of Applied Mathematics of Brown University under the supervision of Professor Chi-Wang Shu. She then spent three years at the Department of Mathematics in Michigan State University and one year at the Scientific Computing and Imaging Institute of The University of Utah as a postdoctoral fellow. Since 2016, she has been with the School of Mathematical Sciences, Zhejiang University as a Special-Termed Professor (2016-2022), and Associate Professor (2023- ). She works in the area of numerical analysis, scientific computing, uncertainty quantification, and machine learning. Her research is focused on the design, analysis and applications of accurate and efficient numerical methods for partial differential equations in various applications.
